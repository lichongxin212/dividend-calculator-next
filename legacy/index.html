FAQs

How to calculate stock dividend yield?

Dividend yield is calculated by dividing the total dividends paid over the past 12 months by the current stock price. For example, if a stock paid $2 in dividends over the last year and currently trades at $50, its dividend yield would be 4% ($2/$50 × 100%).

This calculator automatically fetches the last 12 months of actual dividend payments and current stock prices to provide accurate dividend yields.

How to calculate portfolio dividend yield?

Portfolio dividend yield is calculated by dividing the total annual dividend income by the total portfolio value. The calculator sums up all individual stock positions' dividend income and investment amounts to determine the overall portfolio yield.

For example, if your portfolio value is $10,000 and generates $400 in annual dividends, your portfolio dividend yield would be 4%.

How are monthly dividends calculated?

The monthly dividend chart shows the distribution of dividend payments across the year based on actual ex-dividend dates from the past 12 months. This helps you understand when you'll receive dividend payments and plan your income accordingly.

The calculator only includes dividends that have already been paid (ex-dividend date has passed) to provide accurate income projections.

The calculator uses real-time stock prices and historical dividend data to provide accurate calculations.

What is dividend reinvestment?

Dividend reinvestment (DRIP) is a strategy where you automatically use dividend payments to purchase additional shares of the same stock. This compounds your returns in two ways:

1. Your share count increases with each dividend payment, leading to larger future dividend payments

2. You benefit from both dividend income and potential stock price appreciation on the reinvested shares
